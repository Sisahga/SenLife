Introduction

The elderly population often faces a disparity between their lifespan and healthspan, underscoring the necessity for technologies that enhance their well-being. However, current technologies often lack adaptability and elder-friendly designs, impeding their acceptance and usage among this demographic.

The Problem

Despite a growing proficiency among many elderly individuals in using mobile devices and exploring new technologies, the existing solutions fail to adequately meet their needs. Moreover, the COVID-19 pandemic has exacerbated the need for technologies supporting the well being of the elderly, particularly during periods of social distancing and isolation.

Our Solution

To address these challenges, our design company, Flying Turtles, is developing an app tailored to support the physical and mental well-being of elderly users. This solution, which is focused in simplicity, incorporates big buttons and checklists for ease of use, offers virtual assistance to address technical and health-related issues, provides wellness reminders for activities like walking and hydration, notifies users about local activities, catered to the elderly, and includes a news section curated for their interests and needs.

Research

Surveys

To accurately assess which features would be useful for users of this platform, we have conducted a survey asking six people between the ages of 50 to 80 about their current technology usage habits, preferred devices, and favorite apps.

Quotes from participants

Participant 1 (Age 72): “ I use my smartphone for everything from staying in touch with family to reading news. The app should have clear buttons and easy navigation like my favorite news app.”

Participant 2 (Age 80): “I find technology confusing sometimes. It would be helpful if the app had a virtual assistant to guide me through any problems I encounter.”

Participant 3 (Age 68): “I’m always out and about, so reminders to go for walks and stay hydrated would be great. It would help me stay on track with my daily routine.”

Participant 4 (Age 75): “Since the pandemic, I’ve felt really lonely. It would mean a lot if the app could notify me about local activities for seniors. It could help me connect with others.”

Participant 5 (Age 82): “As someone with poor eyesight, I struggle with small fonts and cluttered screens. The app should have options for larger text and high contrast colors for easier readability.”

Participant 6 (Age 55): “I take care of my elderly parents, and it’s challenging to keep track of their health needs. A feature to set medication reminders and monitor their well-being would be incredibly helpful.”

Personas

Three personas are created to provide a diverse range of user profiles, each with unique goals, frustrations, and preferences, which can help inform the design and development of the web app to meet the needs of different users in Quebec or Montreal.

  • Goals: To stay connected with her grandchildren, manage her health effectively, and find local activities to stay socially engaged.
  • Frustrations: Difficulty navigating complex technology interfaces, feeling isolated from her family due to distance, and managing her chronic health conditions.
  • Bio: Marie-Claire is a retired school teacher living in Montreal. She enjoys spending time with her family, knitting, and attending community events.
  • Myers-Briggs Personality: ISFJ (The Defender)
  • Age: 70
  • Work: Retired
  • Family: Married with two adult children and three grandchildren living in different provinces.
  • Character: Caring, traditional, reliable

  • App Usage:
    • Staying connected with family (4/5).

User Goals

According to the above personas, the key user goals across the personas include staying connected with loved ones, managing health effectively, finding local activities for social engagement, staying mentally stimulated, connecting with like-minded individuals, learning new skills, managing a busy schedule efficiently, maintaining a healthy lifestyle, and staying updated on community events. These goals reflect a diverse range of needs and motivations among users, emphasizing the importance of designing a web app that accommodates various user preferences and aspirations, ultimately enhancing their overall experience and satisfaction with the platform.

User Journey

Using the persona of Fatima Ali, we demonstrate a user journey mapped out using a “Thinks, Does, Feels” framework according to her app usage.

Thinks:

“I hope this app helps me stay organized and on top of my responsibilities.”

Does:

Sets up task lists and reminders for important tasks and deadlines.

Feels:

Feels relieved to have a platform to manage her schedule, reducing her stress and anxiety.

Thinks:

“I should focus on my health and well-being.”

Does:

Sets reminders for hydration and meal times to stay on track with healthy habits.

Feels:

Feels motivated and accountable to stick to her nutrition goals.

Thinks:

“I want to stay connected with my community and join local events.”

Does:

Check the app’s community events calendar to stay updated with local events.

Feels:

Feels connected and engaged with her community.
